    • After finding a love for it in high school, Teddy has performed in a number of different roles, from community theatre to college performances. Of course, with such an expanse of time it's hard to list them all.

    Here are a few highlights:

    2018 Carrie: The Musical - Lawrence D. Cohen

    Teddy played the supporting role of "George" a character in the ensemble of this musical, adapted after the 1980s film.

    2018 We Will Rock You: The Musical - Ben Elton

    Teddy performed in the ensemble of this musical and was a face for the shows marketing materials. The musical tells the story of a renegade group known as the Bohemians who struggle to restore the free exchange of thought and culture in a vaguely Orwellian society.

    2011 The Scene - Theresa Rebeck

    Teddy portrayed the character "Lewis" in this 4 person show, where a young social climber leads an actor into an extramarital affair, setting off a downward spiral of alcoholism and personal ruin for him as his life unravels with his wife leaving for his best friend and his girlfriend leaving him.

    2009 The Doctor in Spite of Himself - Moliere

    I played Leandre in this play with Tidewater Community College,which follows the antics of Sganarelle, a lazy woodcutter who, in a twist of fate, becomes a “doctor.” He tricks Géronte, a pretentious, wealthy socialite, while reuniting his daughter with her forbidden lover.

    2008 Dr. Caligari - Philip Odango

    This play was written and directed by Philip Odango, based on a 1920 film of the same name. Teddy portrayed three separate characters that were defined by their personas not a name. A defiant little brother, a timid doctor's assistant, and a psychotic killer.

    2007 BatBoy: The Musical - Keythe Farley & Bryan Flemming with Laurence O'Keefe

    Teddy performed the character "Ron" and in the ensemble of this comdey/horror musical, about a half boy/half bat creature discovered in a cave in West Virginia and an extreme portrayl of the machinations of being different and trying to fit in.

    Volunteer Show at the Public Library

    Teddy performed in a number of small shows at the local public library in Norfolk, VA.

    Directing

    Teddy is a novice in the world of directing, but he has experienced the joy of seeing a vision brought to life. In high school he directed a small One Act play-written by him-and a few musical endeavors later in college.

    • Teddy has written many things, such as stories, poems and songs. For him, writing has always been a sort of release or a means to quell the storm within, allowing for the expression of complex emotion in a way that simply stating cannot convey. Writing breathes life into thought and dreams, and up until recently was the means he had to illustrate the ever growing world of his imagination.

    Below is a poetic taste.

    An Aching Choir

    An aching choir
    in a song of sorrow
    Belting and melting
    as the hearts and lyrics adorn
    such predicted passions forlorn.
    Contention
    Prevention
    ah...
    tis not much more than a whole of Intention.
    Broken pieces
    tattered creases;
    A sorrowful cadence
    Rupturing
    the frayed remains
    of gentle scars.
    How do we pass the hours?
    The cadenza entices a memory
    Kept so secretly
    Withheld to stay...so...
    peaceful,
    Hateful and vengeful
    Pushing the Forte
    to a momentum of Muerte.
    Let the S i l e n c e
    Settle in
    as the pulse breaks the skin
    A common time tap
    that hits your lap
    and stretches the measure.
    but ah for a quarter of pleasure,
    Let us jump back and repeat.
    For in this,
    One can never double back to the bar line
    for drinks are ever flowing,
    and the tab, needn't be paid.

    Make believe

    Wondering.
    Wondering,
    Wandering...
    how doth time pass so quickly
    where age doth treat you sickly?
    When green presidents are worth naught more than cloth,
    and lives are all but wrought
    and yet are still,
    the same.
    How can we complain,
    tis naught but a rhyme
    from which we are blamed.
    Fabled foes gather more graces
    than we to our own faces.
    For in this displacement
    we seek encouragement,
    though lacking as it is,
    it deters the world where we forgive.
    What happened to our happiness and imagination.
    What happened to a brain
    lacking in hatred
    not one bound by a chain.
    What happened to couches surrounded by moats, swords sheathed by coats, carpet quicksand or linoleum lava.
    What ever happened to a mind that set you free?
    What ever happened to the world of make believe?

    Story Carrier

    A heart is shattered
    a life is lost
    that wasn't long
    must we sing
    this sad song?
    How many times
    will blood be shed?
    How many kids
    will end up dead?
    How much time will lapse
    before truth is spoken?
    How many tears will fall?
    How many worlds broken?
    Can we stand and fight
    for those un-free?
    Can we stand for those
    of merely three?
    A tattered story,
    Cannot be
    All they leave.
    A heart of worry
    Will do nothing more
    Then add a weave
    To lines woven.
    Settle the lore
    breach the barrier
    Save a life
    Don't be just
    A Story Carrier.

    Life Is As It Was

    Life is as it was
    never changing
    stuck in a loop
    of repetition.
    Repeating
    ancestral mistakes
    countries, continents, worlds
    vying
    for control.
    Incommunal powers
    unaware
    of the end
    Of hours
    ah...
    I do despise
    this world so wishfully wise
    the lack of thoughtfulness
    and unending prejudice;
    The changing of times
    has ruptured our powerful minds
    and scorned our vanity
    into pure insanity.
    It's such irony
    how we miss it entirely,
    Proclaiming our prowess
    yet still creating a mess.
    Look into history
    and see people in misery
    and wonder why we follow so closely.
    Powers looking for more,
    cause the downfall of their own doors.
